# polarization — echo chambers across exposure, topology, memory, and topic

## What happens

32 agents are each given a starting stance from 1 to 5 on a polarising question.
They discuss on a Twitter-like feed for 15 steps. Every agent acts every step.
Each turn, an agent reads its timeline and may post, reply, like, or repost, up
to 3 tool calls before it signals FINISHED. Within a step, agents act in
sequence over live state, so a later agent's feed can include posts made earlier
in the same step.

This is the setup from EchoChamberSim (Wang et al., COLING 2025). The three
topics are that paper's topics: euthanasia, AI in key decision-making, and
lifespan extension.

## What we vary

**Signal variables** (the hypotheses are about these):

- **exposure_rule** — five feeds, all rendered by one shared observation
  component (`task_components/observe.py`) so feed format is constant across
  arms.

  | label | feed |
  |---|---|
  | `chrono` | chronological posts from followed accounts |
  | `stance-sim` | followed accounts whose current stance is within 2 of the reader's |
  | `stance-opp` | followed accounts at stance distance 2 or more |
  | `recsys` | TwHIN-BERT recommendation, follower graph ignored |
  | `recsys-rev` | the same ranking served tail-first |

  Current stance is the agent's most recently recorded probe rating, or its
  persona stance before the first probe. The two recsys arms never consult the
  follower graph and serve as null controls for the topology hypothesis.

- **graph_file** — three frozen degree-matched n=32, 64-edge graphs under
  `scenario/assets/graphs/`: `scale-free`, `small-world`, `random`. Generated by
  `tools/gen_graphs.py`.
- **memory_architecture** — `window` (k=20), `none` (no interaction memory), or
  `summary` (short-term plus an LLM-summarised long-term store). Implemented in
  `task_components/agents.py`.
- **topic** — `euthanasia`, `ai-decisions`, `lifespan`. Carried as world
  variants.
- **model** — the four local models (Qwen3.5 4B / 9B / 27B and Gemma-4 31B).

Memory, topic, and model are swept over declared subsets of the exposure × graph
grid rather than the full grid (see `design.yaml`). The robustness variations
run at window memory.

**Design variations** (also swept; whether they matter is for you to test):

- **topic_paraphrase** — the topic statement reworded.
- **persona_content** — the personas re-rendered: paraphrase, narrative stance
  without numeric label, or order shuffle. Produced by
  `tools/gen_polar_personas.py`.
- **prompt_paraphrase** — the action prompt reworded.

## Personas

The roster is controlled, not random. Stance and credulity are balanced (a 16/16
credulity split with equal baseline extremity per group) so the credulity
hypothesis is not confounded by stance assignment. Credulity is stated in each
persona, in a `credulity` field per agent. Demographics (age, gender, education,
occupation) follow the source study's schema and are assigned independently of
stance. Stance wording is topic-generic; the concrete question comes from the
world config.

## Hypotheses

Full statements are in `design.yaml`. In short:

- **h1** — stance-similar feed curation increases opinion clustering relative to
  chronological exposure on the same graph.
- **h2** — under follower-constrained feeds, scale-free and small-world graphs
  develop echo chambers while a degree-matched random graph resists. The recsys
  arms ignore the graph and are the null control.
- **h3** — persistent memory enforces opinion consistency; removing interaction
  memory destabilises expressed opinions, and different architectures anchor
  opinions to different degrees.
- **h4** — credulous personas shift opinion more than skeptical personas under
  identical exposure.
- **h5** — the direction of the exposure-rule effect persists across model scale
  and family.
- **h6** — opposite-stance exposure reduces clustering relative to stance-similar
  curation on the same graph.
- **h7** — the exposure-rule effect holds across the three topics.

We hold back our own measures and predictions until after the deadline.
Choosing how to measure these hypotheses is the task. You may also pose and
test your own questions grounded in this scenario and its data.

## Reading the logs

`probe_events.jsonl` records every agent's 1-5 opinion rating each step as probe
`opinion_rating`. The shipped graph JSON supports network-local analyses of those
ratings. `action_events.jsonl` records posts, replies, and likes with full text,
for stance classification and interaction structure. `prompts_and_responses.jsonl`
includes the summarising agent's memory-compression calls, so memory content is
auditable. The exposure rule, graph, memory, topic, and model for a run are in
`manifest.jsonl`.

## Sweep size

15 grid cells (5 exposure × 3 graphs) × 5 seeds, plus 29 variation conditions ×
5 seeds, for **220 runs** total (about 220k model calls; roughly 1,000 calls per
run at 32 agents × 15 steps).
